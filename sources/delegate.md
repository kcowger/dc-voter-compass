# DC Delegate to Congress (Democratic Primary)

## Race overview

Eleanor Holmes Norton has held the DC delegate seat since 1991 and is not running again. The delegate is a non-voting member of Congress but plays a major role in:

- Pushing for DC statehood
- Defending DC's home rule from federal interference
- Securing federal funding for city projects
- Negotiating with Republicans to limit interference in local affairs
- Symbolic advocacy for treating DC as equivalent to a state in federal programs

The June 16, 2026 Democratic primary winner will face non-Democratic candidates in November. As of source publication, the November field included one Republican and one Statehood Green candidate, with independent candidates able to enter.

## Candidates

Five candidates qualified for the Democratic primary ballot.

## Trent Holbrook

**Age**: 40
**Neighborhood**: Dupont Circle
**Current role**: Recently departed legislative staffer to Congresswoman Norton (8 years)

### Stated priorities

- Continue Norton's statehood push as a national priority
- Defend home rule and expand it
- Advocate for DC to be treated as a state for federal programs

### Specific positions and arguments

- Argues that experience in Norton's office gives him knowledge of "what moment we're in, what's moving, and what we could attach to it" in the legislative process
- Argues Council members lack the right kind of experience because "this position is not like any position in government"
- Asserts Norton remained "one of the most effective lawmakers in American history" despite questions about her final years

### Endorsements

Not specifically documented in source material. Norton has not endorsed a successor as of source publication.

### Inferred profile

- **Theory of the job (inferred)**: Continuity with Norton's institutional approach. Inside game.
- **Background type**: Hill staffer / institutional knowledge
- **Strategy**: Inside game, relationships, process knowledge

### Strengths

- Deepest Hill experience in the field
- Direct continuity on active DC legislative battles
- Lower learning curve on Day 1

### Weaknesses to flag

- Tied closely to Norton's approach at a moment when there are real questions about whether that approach was still working
- No independent legislative record
- Limited public profile in DC
- No Council experience

## Greg Jaczko

**Age**: 55
**Neighborhood**: Tenleytown
**Current role**: Runs a consulting firm on renewable energy
**Background**: PhD physicist. Former chairman of the Nuclear Regulatory Commission under Obama. 30 years in DC.

### Stated priorities

- Push back on the Trump administration's treatment of federal workers
- Get DC exempted from federal taxation as leverage for statehood
- Push to be appointed to the House Oversight Committee

### Specific positions and arguments

- **Signature idea**: Reframe DC's fight as "no taxation until we get representation." Push legislation to exempt DC from federal taxes (similar to Puerto Rico). In 2024 DC paid $45 billion in federal taxes, more per capita than any state.
- Argues this would also draw corporations to DC for tax reasons, growing the local economy
- Argues it would reverse the national framing and increase pressure for statehood
- Notes that the Heritage Foundation has at times supported ending federal taxation for DC residents

### Endorsements

Not specifically documented in source material.

### Inferred profile

- **Theory of the job (inferred)**: Outside game. Reframe the debate via pressure tactics.
- **Background type**: Outside politics; federal regulatory expertise
- **Strategy**: Confrontational leverage

### Strengths

- Genuinely novel strategic framing in a field of mostly continuity candidates
- Strong federal executive branch experience
- Credible advocate for the federal workforce, which is currently a major DC economic issue

### Weaknesses to flag

- No legislative experience
- The "no taxation" framing depends on building enough national pressure to force action; that's an outside-game theory that may not match the current congressional environment
- No DC Council or local government experience
- Limited public profile in DC

### Source

The 51st delegate race profile, May 11, 2026.

## Brooke Pinto

**Age**: 33
**Neighborhood**: Logan Circle (Ward 2)
**Current role**: Ward 2 Councilmember (since 2020)
**Background**: Connecticut native

### Stated priorities

- Push for statehood and protect city autonomy
- Use federal leverage on housing (top stated priority)
- Public safety (her area of focus on Council)

### Specific positions and arguments

- Repeal the federal Height Act to allow taller buildings in DC
- Speed transfer of old federal buildings and land to local control for affordable housing
- Declare more federal enterprise and opportunity zones in DC
- On public safety: cites her Council work on penalties for gun crimes, expanded pre-trial detention, teen curfew zones
- Argues housing affordability is the top concern of Black residents she's heard from, due to displacement pressures

### Endorsements

- Has raised over $1.2 million, more than any other delegate candidate (source: The 51st)
- Specific endorsement list not detailed in source material

### Inferred profile

- **Theory of the job (inferred)**: Use federal levers for tangible local policy wins. Practical/transactional rather than symbolic.
- **Background type**: DC Council; legislative experience
- **Strategy**: Mixed; more policy-execution than relational

### Strengths

- Active legislative record on the Council
- Top fundraiser
- Specific concrete policy proposals tied to federal mechanisms

### Weaknesses to flag

- **Major character flag from source**: Pinto's campaign published unredacted opposition research on Robert White and his family. She apologized but refused to drop out when he asked. This is a real concern for a job that runs on relationships and trust.
- Connecticut native; has faced repeated criticism that she doesn't know DC well enough
- Her public safety record is contested by criminal justice reform advocates
- Limited Hill experience

### Source

The 51st delegate race profile, May 11, 2026.

## Robert White

**Age**: 44
**Neighborhood**: Native Washingtonian
**Current role**: At-Large Councilmember (10 years)
**Background**: Legislative counsel to Eleanor Holmes Norton, 2008 to 2013. Lost 2022 mayoral primary.

### Stated priorities

- DC statehood (unique historical opportunity right now)
- Stabilize DC's economy post-federal-workforce cuts
- Pursue a seat on House Appropriations Committee specifically

### Specific positions and arguments

- Wants Appropriations seat to direct federal funds to DC and head off Republican budget riders
- Push to transfer federal lands around L'Enfant Plaza to local control
- Coordinate with regional partners on federal payments to offset federal workforce cuts
- Puerto Rico-style tax model: businesses moving to DC don't pay federal taxes on income earned in DC
- Argues he understands DC personally and professionally better than competitors

### Endorsements (per The 51st)

- Congressional Black Caucus
- Congressional Progressive Caucus
- Local unions
- Council colleagues including Janeese Lewis George and Charles Allen
- Senator Elizabeth Warren

### Inferred profile

- **Theory of the job (inferred)**: Inside game with progressive coalition. Appropriations focus.
- **Background type**: Combined DC Council and Hill experience
- **Strategy**: Inside game; relationships and process

### Strengths

- Only candidate with both substantial DC Council experience (10 years) AND substantial Hill experience (5 years on Norton's staff)
- Targeting Appropriations is the single highest-leverage committee assignment for a non-voting delegate seeking to move federal money
- Strongest published endorsement list of the field
- Native Washingtonian; only Black candidate in the race
- Substantial coalition: CBC + CPC is unusual breadth

### Weaknesses to flag

- **Real character flag**: Posted a social media joke about the shooting at the White House Correspondents Dinner; later deleted and apologized, calling it "inappropriate and insensitive."
- Fundraising lags Pinto and Zalesne
- Lost 2022 mayoral primary; head-to-head citywide track record is mixed

### Source

The 51st delegate race profile, May 11, 2026.

## Kinney Zalesne

**Age**: 59
**Neighborhood**: Cleveland Park
**Current role**: Democratic fundraiser
**Background**: Federal government, nonprofit leadership focused on DC kids getting to college, Microsoft, recent prominent fundraiser for the Democratic Party

### Stated priorities

- Autonomy (statehood and steps toward it)
- Economy (reduce DC's dependence on the federal government)

### Specific positions and arguments

- Call a "statehood summit" after the June 16 primary to refine messaging and strategy
- Launch a national advertising campaign with messaging targeting DC visitors (possible tagline: "Welcome to D.C., we're the last colony")
- Create a Capitol Caucus in the House made of representatives from surrounding states to diversify the regional economy
- Argues the delegate role's power comes from the person, not the position, and that her national networks are uniquely suited

### Endorsements (per The 51st)

- Three sitting members of the US House (specific names not in source)
- Raised over $660,000

### Inferred profile

- **Theory of the job (inferred)**: National coalition-building and strategic messaging. The campaign-strategist theory of the delegate role.
- **Background type**: National political/fundraising networks; outside politics professionally
- **Strategy**: Coalition building and public messaging

### Strengths

- Strongest national political network in the field
- Treats statehood explicitly as a campaign to be won, with messaging discipline and metrics
- Significant fundraising; ran an early six-figure advertising campaign

### Weaknesses to flag

- No legislative experience
- No DC Council experience
- No Hill staff experience
- Limited public profile in DC before this race

The 51st delegate race profile, May 11, 2026.

## Framework dimensions for this race

The tool should ask users to weight these dimensions, then score candidates:

### Dimension 1: Most important job of the next delegate

- A) Push statehood meaningfully closer, even if it takes years
- B) Defend DC's existing autonomy from current federal attacks
- C) Direct federal money and rebuild DC's economy post-workforce cuts
- D) Use federal levers for tangible local wins (housing, public safety)

### Dimension 2: Style of advocacy

- A) Inside game: process knowledge, relationships, knowing what fights to pick
- B) Outside game: confrontational leverage, public pressure, reframe the debate
- C) Coalition building: national networks, elite ties, messaging campaigns
- D) Local mobilization: rooted in DC, organizes residents

### Dimension 3: Background

- A) Hill experience (worked directly on DC issues in Congress)
- B) DC Council experience (legislative record, local accountability)
- C) Outside-politics expertise (federal agency, business, nonprofit)
- D) National political/fundraising networks
