# DC Mayor (Democratic Primary)

## Race overview

Mayor Muriel Bowser announced in November 2025 that she would not seek a fourth term, opening the first contested mayoral primary in 20 years. The Democratic primary is **June 16, 2026**, and in heavily Democratic DC the primary winner is widely expected to win the November general election.

Eight Democratic candidates have qualified for the ballot. Two have consolidated front-runner status by qualifying for the Fair Elections public financing program and raising over $1 million each: **Janeese Lewis George** (Ward 4 Councilmember, progressive) and **Kenyan McDuffie** (former At-Large Councilmember, moderate). This guide focuses on those two; the other six candidates are not covered here in depth.

**Ranked-choice voting applies for the first time in this race.**

## Janeese Lewis George

**Age**: 38
**Neighborhood**: Ward 4 (third-generation Washingtonian)
**Current role**: Ward 4 Councilmember since January 2021
**Background**: B.A. St. John's University, J.D. Howard Law School. Former Assistant Attorney General under Karl Racine; former Assistant General Counsel at OSSE. Self-identified democratic socialist; the first Council member affiliated with the Metro DC Democratic Socialists of America since 1998.

### Stated priorities

Lewis George's platform is organized around eleven "for all" planks. The core themes are affordability (housing, childcare, utilities), economic populism, and standing up to the Trump administration.

### Housing

- **Target: 72,000 new units over five years.** This is roughly six times McDuffie's pace.
- **"Dignified Homes DC"**: a new social housing program for publicly owned, mixed-income housing with stable rents. She has not specified whether DC Housing Authority would manage these projects.
- **Funding mechanism**: has proposed using "billions of dollars" from the city's pension funds to finance affordable housing construction. This proposal has drawn editorial criticism from the Washington Post.
- **Zoning reform**: reform zoning laws and streamline permitting to enable more building, particularly near Metro, schools, and parks.
- **Rent and tenant protections**: strengthen and expand DC's rent stabilization program; strengthen the Tenant Opportunity to Purchase Act (TOPA); create an ombudsman for public housing residents.
- **Voted against the RENTAL Act** in September 2025 (one of three "no" votes on the 10-3 supermajority). The RENTAL Act made it cheaper to build affordable housing and easier to evict tenants substantiated as violent. She tried to remove the violent-tenant eviction provision; her amendment failed 8-5.

### Public safety

- **Framework**: "prevention, intervention, and enforcement," with the entire government playing a role rather than just MPD.
- **Community Hubs in all eight wards** to centralize family services, job programs, and mental health resources.
- **No stated hiring target** for new MPD officers. Platform language commits to addressing MPD's vacancy through expanding the cadet program, reducing forced overtime, renovating facilities, and providing childcare benefits.
- **History on police funding**: wrote a 2021 letter calling on Mayor Bowser to "reallocate funding" from MPD and criticized adding 135 officers.
- **Youth curfew**: voted against extending curfew four times (November 2025, December 2025, February 2026, May 2026). Has said she would end the policy as mayor. Did vote in favor of the July 2025 unanimous bill that tightened the curfew as part of a broader public safety package.
- **Pre-trial detention**: was the lone vote against the 2023 Prioritizing Public Safety Emergency Amendment Act, which expanded judicial discretion to hold violent crime suspects pre-trial.
- **Eviction of violent tenants**: was the only no vote on the 2021 Eviction Moratorium Public Safety Exception (passed 12-1).

### Childcare

- **Universal childcare subsidy**, expanding the existing program so no family pays more than 7% of income on care. This is the federal HHS affordability benchmark.
- **Mechanism**: subsidy paid directly to providers; families pay capped share at point of service.

### Taxes and economy

- **Co-authored a 2021 income tax increase** on high earners as part of DC's FY22 budget.
- **Supports a Business Activity Tax** during her mayoral campaign.
- **Downtown Development Corporation**: a new entity modeled on the 1980s Pennsylvania Avenue Development Corporation to convert obsolete offices to housing, expand downtown universities, and unlock the air-rights project over Amtrak tracks behind Union Station.

### Utilities

- Introduced 2026 emergency legislation to pause Pepco shutoffs for customers owing $1,000 or less.
- 10-point utility affordability plan including expanded solar on government buildings, accountability for high-load data centers, on-bill financing for home energy upgrades, and new Public Service Commission appointments.

### Federal relations and ICE

- "No comply in advance" framing on the Trump administration.
- **Would end MPD cooperation with ICE.** Introduced the Safe Community Places and Policing Amendment Act of 2025 to prohibit MPD from cooperating with immigration enforcement unless a warrant requires it.
- Has said she would rescind the MPD order permitting officers to work with ICE on day one.

### Education

- Supports expanded mental health services for students, hiring more teachers, focus on chronic absenteeism.
- **Proposes changing mayoral control of schools** to give the nine-member State Board of Education authority to appoint and oversee the State Superintendent of Education.
- Supports de-emphasizing how much students learn over a year in teacher performance evaluations.

### Transportation

- Supports **congestion pricing** in DC.
- Supports raising property tax rates around Metro stations and bus routes to fund WMATA.

### Endorsements

Lewis George has consolidated **labor unions and progressive groups**, including a broad cross-section of DC's labor movement. Source: Axios DC, May 2026.

### Polling

Per her campaign site as of May 2026, Lewis George leads McDuffie by double digits in both the multi-candidate field and in ranked-choice voting simulations. (Source: her campaign; independent polling should be verified before publishing this number.)

### Ethics flags to disclose

- **DC Office of Campaign Finance investigation** (active as of April 2026) into alleged improper campaign coordination with organized labor groups.
- **May 2026 complaint** filed by the Campaign for Accountability, a nonprofit watchdog, with the DC Board of Ethics and Government Accountability alleging she failed to properly disclose her family's financial assets on mandatory Personal Financial Disclosure Statements over multiple years.
- **May 2026**: in public comments she referred to expelled Ward 8 Councilmember Trayon White (under federal bribery indictment) as a "mentor." She had earlier agreed to administer his oath of office after no DC judge would do so. She did vote with the Council supermajority to expel him.

## Kenyan McDuffie

**Age**: 51
**Background**: Wilson High School, UDC, B.A. from Howard, J.D. from University of Maryland Law. Earlier career as a postal worker, aide to Delegate Eleanor Holmes Norton, civil rights attorney at U.S. DOJ under Obama. **Elected to DC Council representing Ward 5 in 2012**, served through 2022, then **elected At-Large in 2022.** Stepped down from At-Large seat to run for mayor.

### Stated priorities

Lower cost of living, expand opportunity in every ward, strengthen public safety. Campaign frame is making DC "the most affordable city in the U.S."

### Housing

- **Target: 12,000 new units by 2030**, preserve 20,000 affordable units by 2030, deliver 1,500 family-sized affordable units by 2035.
- **Pledge to cut approval timelines by 50%** and double the number of first-time DC homebuyers in five years.
- **Voted YES on the RENTAL Act** (10-3 majority, September 2025), which made it cheaper to build affordable housing and easier to evict tenants substantiated as violent.
- **Expanded down-payment assistance** and streamlined regulatory approvals for new residential construction.
- **Homelessness**: smaller non-congregate shelters; reform Rapid Rehousing to prevent "premature exits"; improved one-stop service centers for public benefits.

### Public safety

- **Hire 1,000 additional MPD officers** to reduce overtime costs and address crime, bringing the force back to roughly its size during the early 2010s homicide low.
- Strengthening officer retention, supporting cadet program for DC residents joining MPD.
- **Authored the NEAR Act** (Neighborhood Engagement Achieves Results), which created DC's violence intervention program and co-response teams with the Department of Behavioral Health.
- **Authored DC's police body-worn camera law.**
- **Voted in favor of youth curfew three times** (July 2025, November 2025, December 2025). Has said curfews "cannot be a standalone fix" but are part of a broader strategy. Argues teen takeovers should be treated "like the public safety emergency that they are."
- **Pre-trial detention**: voted for the 2023 Public Safety Emergency Amendment Act expanding detention provisions. In 2024 authored an amendment requiring the expanded detention provisions to sunset after 225 days to allow study of whether they reduced violent crime.

### Childcare

- **Expand the Local Child Tax Credit** to help families afford childcare.
- **Employer incentives** to expand childcare benefits for employees.
- Expand childcare supply through zoning changes and use of District-owned space.
- Position is that universal subsidy is too expensive; targeted tax credit is fiscally realistic.

### Taxes and economy

- **No broad-based tax increases.** Affordability platform emphasizes lowering costs without raising taxes.
- **Capital One Arena and RFK stadium deals**: McDuffie helped secure these, paired with community benefits and DC-hiring commitments. The RFK deal is contested by some progressive critics.
- Workforce strategy targets **AI, cybersecurity, healthcare, hospitality, and trades** as growth sectors. "Growth with guardrails" approach to AI's workforce impacts.
- **"Stay in DC" fund** to prevent small-business displacement.

### Utilities

- **"DC Utility Help Center"** with automatic enrollment in energy discount programs.
- Stronger shutoff protections.
- Funding for resident home repairs aimed at reducing energy costs.
- Note: as chair of Council's Business and Economic Development Committee for nine years, McDuffie oversaw the Public Service Commission, Pepco, and Washington Gas; enacted pandemic-era customer protections.

### Federal relations and ICE

- **Would end ICE raids in the District** and revoke MPD cooperation with ICE. Has defended DC's sanctuary status. Quote: "The ICE raids have to end."
- States he will approach the White House with a focus on protecting Home Rule and "fighting smart and strategically."

### Education

- **"Every Child, Every Step"** program supporting students throughout their educational journey.
- **Supports continued mayoral leadership of DC schools** (opposite of Lewis George's position).
- Career pathway investments aligned with UDC and adult workforce programs.
- "Future DC Jobs" Fellowship with private-sector placements.
- Out-of-school-time programming expansion across all wards.

### Transportation

- Favors raising parking rates.
- Protecting affordability of transit.
- Improving cost-effective alternatives to car ownership.

### Endorsements

McDuffie is backed by **real-estate and business-friendly organizations**, including Opportunity DC. Per Axios DC May 2026: a "business- and real estate-focused bloc, attracted to his promises of economic growth and support for public safety measures."

### Ethics flags to disclose

- **No active ethics investigations** as of source date.
- McDuffie **chaired the Ad Hoc Committee** that investigated Ward 8 Councilmember Trayon White after his 2024 federal bribery arrest. The committee's independent investigation found substantial evidence of rule violations; the Council voted to expel White. McDuffie said he was "confident in the investigation and the report."

## Framework dimensions for the mayor race

### Dimension 1: Scale of ambition vs. fiscal sustainability

- A) Major new programs (universal childcare, social housing, expanded services) are worth funding even with hard tradeoffs
- B) Smaller, fiscally cautious steps are more realistic given DC's budget pressure
- C) Mixed: ambitious on some issues, cautious on others

### Dimension 2: Public safety approach

- A) Prevention-first: invest in jobs, mental health, youth services; less aggressive enforcement
- B) Enforcement-first: hire more police, support curfews, expand detention
- C) Balanced: enforcement plus prevention, with current MPD size

### Dimension 3: Federal/Trump posture

- A) Confrontational: refuse to comply, draw lines clearly
- B) Strategic: fight smart, pick battles, protect home rule through negotiation
- C) Both candidates roughly align here on most specifics (notably ICE)

### Dimension 4: Housing priority

- A) Build dramatically more, including publicly owned social housing
- B) Build at a sustainable pace with strong tenant protections and ownership pathways
- C) Focus on preservation and affordability of existing stock

### Dimension 5: Taxes and revenue

- A) Progressive tax increases on wealthy and corporations to fund expanded services
- B) No broad tax increases; lower cost of living through targeted programs and efficiency

### Candidate fit matrix

| Dimension | Lewis George best fits | McDuffie best fits |
|-----------|------------------------|--------------------|
| Scale of ambition | A | B |
| Public safety | A | B |
| Federal posture | A | B (mostly aligned with A on specifics) |
| Housing priority | A | B |
| Taxes and revenue | A | B |

### Recommendation logic

This race is unusually clean ideologically. Almost every dimension splits Lewis George (A) from McDuffie (B). Voters who answer mostly A should rank Lewis George #1; voters who answer mostly B should rank McDuffie #1.

The interesting cases are:

- **Progressive on policy but uncomfortable with ethics flags**: Lewis George's policy positions match a progressive voter, but the OCF investigation, BEGA complaint, and the Trayon White "mentor" comment are real flags. The tool should surface these honestly.
- **Moderate on policy but uncomfortable with business-aligned governance**: McDuffie's coalition is heavily real-estate and business; voters who want progressive governance but worry about Lewis George's specific positions (pension-fund housing financing, no MPD hiring target) may be in a tough spot.
- **Single-issue voters on public safety**: McDuffie is the clearer choice on enforcement scale; Lewis George on prevention emphasis.
- **Single-issue voters on housing supply**: Lewis George's target is six times McDuffie's, but her social housing mechanism is unproven at DC scale. McDuffie's plan is smaller but builds on existing tools.
