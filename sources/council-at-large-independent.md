# DC Council At-Large (Independent Special Election)

## Race overview

This is a special election to serve out the rest of Kenyan McDuffie's At-Large term, ending January 2027. McDuffie stepped down from the seat to run for mayor. A separate race in November 2026 will determine who serves the next full four-year term.

**Important distinction**: This is not a primary. All registered DC voters can vote in this race, regardless of party registration. It appears on the back of page two of mail-in ballots, which makes it easy to miss.

**Ranked-choice voting applies.**

## Strategic dynamics

Two of the three candidates, Doni Crawford and Jacque Patterson, have cross-endorsed each other. They are explicitly asking their supporters to rank the other one second. This is a strategic move designed to push Elissa Silverman out under RCV rules.

The tool should make this dynamic visible to users. If a user's first-choice candidate is Silverman, their #2 ranking carries no penalty under RCV (only activates if Silverman is eliminated). If a user's first-choice candidate is Crawford or Patterson, their #2 ranking of the other cross-endorser is part of the strategic alliance against Silverman.

## Doni Crawford

**Age**: 36
**Neighborhood**: DC (specific neighborhood not in source)
**Current role**: Interim At-Large Councilmember (appointed to fill McDuffie's seat)
**Background**: Originally from Pittsburgh. Moved to DC in 2019 to work as a policy analyst at the DC Fiscal Policy Institute. Joined McDuffie's council office in 2022 as senior policy advisor; rose to director of the Committee on Business and Economic Development.

### Stated priorities

- Youth investment (out-of-school programming, behavioral health clinicians in schools, extended rec center hours)
- Housing supply (as a renter, references this directly)
- Helping seniors pass homes to next generation

### Specific positions

- Proposed amendments to teen curfew bill (timing of police approach to youth, programming requirements during curfew periods, sunset of zones by end of 2028)
- Worked on the RFK stadium deal as a McDuffie staffer
- Worked on putting funds toward a direct cash assistance program
- Self-describes as a consensus builder willing to find middle ground on stuck issues

### Endorsements (per The 51st)

- Associations for DC's realtors
- Restaurant Association of Metropolitan Washington
- Opportunity DC (pro-business group)

### Cross-endorsement

- Cross-endorsed with Jacque Patterson; they are asking supporters to rank each other second

### Inferred profile

- **Ideological lean (inferred)**: Moderate. The Realtors plus Restaurant Association plus Opportunity DC endorsement bundle is a clear business-aligned coalition. Background at DC Fiscal Policy Institute (a progressive think tank) complicates this slightly, but her direct working relationship with McDuffie (the moderate mayoral candidate) and the endorsement coalition are the stronger recent signals.
- **Background type**: Government insider via interim seat and four years as McDuffie's senior staff

### Strengths

- Currently sitting on the council; has actual voting record and a track record of legislating during her interim tenure
- Real consensus-building example via the curfew amendment work
- DC Fiscal Policy Institute background suggests budget literacy
- Combined policy think-tank plus council staff experience is unusual

### Weaknesses to flag

- Clearly aligned with McDuffie's politics; voters seeking a progressive shift will see this as continuity with the moderate wing
- Newer to DC (arrived 2019); shorter local roots than other candidates
- Cross-endorsement strategy means a vote for Crawford strategically helps the anti-Silverman alliance

## Jacque Patterson

**Age**: 61
**Neighborhood**: Shipley Terrace (Ward 8)
**Current role**: President and At-Large member of the DC State Board of Education (SBOE)
**Background**: Stationed at Fort Andrews Air Base in the mid-1990s; bought a Ward 8 townhouse and stayed. Five terms (10 years) as an ANC commissioner. Roles include community affairs coordinator under Mayor Anthony Williams, program and policy analyst at DC Health under Vincent Gray, work at two charter schools, work at nonprofits including the Federal City Council and Martha's Table. Ran unsuccessfully for school board in 2007, Ward 8 Council in 2012, SBOE in 2016, before finally winning the At-Large SBOE seat in 2020.

### Stated priorities

- Education (top priority, especially in budget process)
- Public safety and youth well-being
- Affordable housing

### Specific positions

- Wants more community schools that serve as "one-stop shops" with services for students and families
- Supports curfew zones but only temporarily, paired with investment in teen programming
- Wants denser, mixed-income housing; cites the Barry Farm redevelopment as a model
- Wants to increase the Housing Production Trust Fund to **$250 million per year** (typically funded at $100 million; Bowser proposed a 50% cut in her most recent budget)

### Endorsements (per The 51st)

- ElectED DC (education equity group)

### Cross-endorsement

- Cross-endorsed with Doni Crawford; they are asking supporters to rank each other second

### Inferred profile

- **Ideological lean (inferred)**: Center-left to moderate. Career under Williams and Gray (moderate mayors), cross-endorsement with the more clearly moderate Crawford. The $250M HPTF target is a progressive housing position but the overall coalition is moderate.
- **Background type**: Government, education, and nonprofit insider; deep Ward 8 community roots

### Strengths

- Strong, specific affordable housing target ($250M HPTF) is the most ambitious housing financing position in this race
- Deep Ward 8 roots; lived in Shipley Terrace for decades
- Diverse career across government, education, and nonprofits
- Currently leads SBOE; familiar with citywide elected office
- Pairs accountability framing on teen behavior with programming investment

### Weaknesses to flag

- Cross-endorsement with Crawford signals he's positioning to the right of Silverman ideologically
- Lost three previous races (2007, 2012, 2016) before winning SBOE in 2020; head-to-head track record is mixed
- Less direct council legislative experience than Crawford (interim seat) or Silverman (8 years)
- Education-first framing may concern voters who want a broader policy lane

## Elissa Silverman

**Age**: 53
**Neighborhood**: Capitol Hill
**Current role**: Running for the seat she held 2015 to 2022
**Background**: Eight years as an At-Large councilmember. Lost the seat to Kenyan McDuffie in 2022. Spent months after the 2022 election fighting Office of Campaign Finance complaints (one initially ruled she misused public campaign funds; by April 2023 all complaints were dismissed). She characterized the complaints as a "smear campaign." Worked in Maryland Gov. Wes Moore's administration as chief strategy officer in the state's Department of Labor in 2024 for nine months before leaving.

### Stated priorities

1. **Trump and federal interference**: Wants the council to do a "Trump analysis" any time the city acts in ways that might trigger White House or congressional pushback; wants coordination with the AG's office and the congressional delegate
2. **Housing**: Wants more coordination across DC's housing tools (vouchers, Housing Production Trust Fund, private partnerships, DCHA) with a unified "north star" of goals; interested in working with DCHA to redevelop public land for mixed-use communities
3. **Truancy**: Wants to address the nearly 40% student absenteeism rate by working with DCPS chancellor on school environment; describes truancy as a "house-on-fire issue"

### Specific past legislative accomplishments (as a sitting councilmember)

- Lead on passing DC's paid family leave law (passed over Mayor Bowser's opposition; veto-proof Council majority)
- Helped create a maintenance and repair fund for public housing
- History of holding city agencies accountable

### Endorsements (per The 51st)

- Multiple labor unions
- Sierra Club DC
- Bike Walk Bus PAC
- Greater Greater Washington (DC's YIMBY-aligned urbanist group, per The 51st's election guide series)

### Stated alignment

- Says she aligns more with Janeese Lewis George (progressive) than with McDuffie on the mayoral race, but says she'd want McDuffie to succeed in office if he won

### Inferred profile

- **Ideological lean (inferred)**: Progressive. Labor + Sierra Club + Bike Walk Bus + GGW + self-stated alignment with Lewis George is a strong progressive signal cluster.
- **Background type**: Government insider with 8 years of legislative experience; the only candidate with substantial DC Council voting record

### Strengths

- Most council experience of any candidate in this race (8 years)
- Track record of actual legislative wins, including passing paid family leave over mayoral opposition
- Oversight is her signature lane; she explicitly entered the race because residents said the council wasn't holding agencies accountable
- Clear progressive coalition
- GGW endorsement is a strong pro-housing-supply signal
- Coordinated housing tools framing addresses a real fragmentation problem in DC housing policy

### Weaknesses to flag

- Lost in 2022, so has been beaten in a head-to-head citywide race recently
- The 2023 Office of Campaign Finance complaints (all eventually dismissed) created a year of bad press; the issue remains in the public record even though she was cleared
- Acknowledges an adversarial relationship with Bowser during her previous tenure (especially on paid family leave); her promise to avoid that going forward is a course correction that voters can weigh
- Left the Wes Moore administration after only nine months; voters may want to understand why

## Framework dimensions for this race

Same as the At-Large Democratic primary race (this is the second At-Large seat), but with a smaller field.

### Dimension 1: Ideological direction

- A) Progressive lean
- B) Centrist
- C) Doesn't matter

### Dimension 2: Most important issue

- A) Housing supply
- B) Education
- C) Federal/Trump resilience
- D) Oversight of DC agencies
- E) Public safety / youth

### Dimension 3: Candidate profile

- A) Past council experience
- B) Education-system insider
- C) Current council insider (newer)
- D) Community-rooted Ward 8 voice

### Candidate fit matrix

| Candidate | Best on Dim 1 | Best on Dim 2 | Best on Dim 3 |
|-----------|---------------|---------------|---------------|
| Crawford | B | A, E | C |
| Patterson | B | A, B, E | B + D |
| Silverman | A | A, C, D | A |

### Recommendation logic for this race

**For voters who prioritize progressive lean + oversight + housing supply**: Silverman is the dominant fit. She's the only progressive in the field and she covers the housing supply and oversight angles.

**For voters who prioritize affordable housing financing and education**: Patterson's $250M HPTF target and education background may fit.

**For voters who prioritize business-aligned governance and consensus-building**: Crawford is the clearest fit.

### Ranked-choice voting strategy notes

The tool should explain to users:

1. RCV means your second choice only activates if your first choice is eliminated
2. Ranking a candidate second does not hurt your first choice
3. The Crawford-Patterson cross-endorsement means voters who pick either of them as #1 are encouraged to pick the other as #2, which is a coordinated effort against Silverman
4. A voter whose first choice is Silverman should think carefully about whether to rank anyone second; if they have a clear next-best, they should rank them; if not, leaving the #2 slot blank is also valid
5. This race is easy to miss on the ballot; mention this explicitly
